Denver IEEE Computer Society Guest Lecturer

Dr. Robert Whitman

  • Professor of Electrical and Computer Engineering
  • Department of Electrical & Computer Engineering, University of Denver
Dr. Whitman has earned a BS in Electrical Engineering from the University of New Mexico (UNM) and an MS and PhD in Electrical Engineering (specializing in Digital Signal Processing) from Colorado State University, 1996. Dr. Whitman proudly earned the titles of "the first member of the Navajo Nation to earn a Ph.D. in an engineering discipline and the first to earn professorship of engineering in University." Dr. Whitman has worked for the IBM Corporation in San Jose California, then transferred to the IBM facility in Boulder Colorado. In 1996, Dr. Whitman was appointed as an Assistant Professor of Electrical and Computer Engineering at the University of New Mexico. In 1998, Dr. Whitman was appointed as an assistant professor at the University of Denver (DU) where he taught electrical, computer and general engineering courses at DU until his retirement in June 2021, where he has served as the associate chairman of the Department of ECE in 2007-2009, and director of ECE undergraduate programs. Additionally, Dr. Whitman developed several new courses which were incorporated into the DU engineering undergraduate curriculum.In November 2014, Dr. Whitman was elected Fellow of AAAS (American Association for the Advancement of Science). Dr. Whitman is also a respected life member of the Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers (IEEE), IEEE Signal Processing Society, the Acoustical Society of America, and the American Indian Science and Engineering Society (AISES) where he was awarded and served as a long-time member, Sequoyah member, and a past board of directors member (1983-1986, and 2001-2005). Within the American Indian Science and Engineering Society (AISES) Dr. Whitman has additionally been awarded the highest honors of "the Ely S. Parker Award" from AISES in 2010. Dr. Whitman has additionally, served as science fair judge in the National American Indian Science and Engineering Fair (NAISEF) and International Science and Engineering Fair (ISEF), and the president of the AISES Publishing Inc (2001 to 2005), which publishes the Winds of Change magazine.
